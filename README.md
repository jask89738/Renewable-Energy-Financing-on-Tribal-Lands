Project Development Phases
Based on the DOE framework for developing and financing renewable energy projects on tribal lands.

Phase 1
Resource Assessment
Evaluate solar, wind, and other renewable energy potential using NREL data and on-site measurements.

3–6 months
Phase 2
Feasibility Study
Conduct detailed technical and economic feasibility analysis, including grid interconnection studies.

6–12 months
Phase 3
Project Development
Secure land rights, permits, power purchase agreements, and finalize engineering designs.

12–24 months
Phase 4
Financing & Closing
Structure debt, tax equity, and grant financing. Complete financial close with all stakeholders.

6–12 months
Phase 5
Construction
Build the renewable energy facility with tribal workforce development and local hiring priorities.

12–18 months
Phase 6
Operations & Maintenance
Ongoing operation, monitoring, and maintenance with tribal capacity building for long-term management.

20–30 years
